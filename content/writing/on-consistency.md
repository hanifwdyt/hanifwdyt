The hardest part isn't starting. It's showing up on day 47 when the novelty has worn off and no one's watching.

I used to think consistency was about disciplineâ€”forcing yourself to do something you don't want to do. But that's exhausting. Real consistency is about building systems where showing up is easier than not showing up.

Swimming taught me this. I don't swim because I'm disciplined. I swim because I've removed every excuse: my bag is packed the night before, the pool is on my route, my calendar blocks the time. The decision is made before I wake up.

Consistency isn't a personality trait. It's architecture.
